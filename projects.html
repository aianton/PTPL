    • PROJECTS

    There are three projects this semester. Each project will result in a written report that must conform to the 8.5" x 11", Two-Column IEEE Conference Proceedings Format IEEE CS publication standards. The Ethics Project will be done either individually or in pairs chosen by the students. The other two projects will be done in groups assigned based in part on student responses to the survey conducted on the first day of class. The Final Exam for this class will be replaced by an oral presentation of the Re-Identification project.

    Book Report

    The required book for this course is a novel, entitled The Circle, by Dave Eggers.  Students are expected to write a brief book report, 3 pages in the IEEE format.  More guidance will be provided in the coming weeks.

    This project will be due February 13, 2014.

    PROJECT #1 -- LITERATURE SURVEY

    The literature survey should be a survey of research in some particular area of Privacy, Technology, Policy, and Law. Students should discuss their chosen topic with the professor before commencing the survey. Students can pursue this project in several ways. For example, a student may choose two or three papers describing different research projects that have tackled a similar problem, and write a detailed comparison of them or a student may choose a larger set of papers that cover the topic, and write an overview, discussing both what has been achieved and what remaining problems there are. Other approaches are possible and the instructor will be available to provide feedback along the way.

    The deliverable for this project is a six page report to be completed in groups assigned by the instructors. The project will be due on February 28th, 2014.

    PROJECT #2 -- FLEXIBLE

    Because of the multi-disiplinary nature of this course, Project #2 will be decided based upon students prefer to work in teams or alone.  

    The deliverable for this project will be a report consisting of a minimum of five pages and a maximum of 6 pages, excluding appendicies. The project is due on April 10, 2014.

    PROJECT PRESENTATIONS

    During the final examination period, each student will give a presentation in which they describe their practical project. Length of presentations will depend upon course enrollment. Slides for each presentation must be submitted by 6:00 pm on the day prior to the presentations on T-Square.
